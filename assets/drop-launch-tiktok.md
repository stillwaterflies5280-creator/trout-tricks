# Drop launch — TikTok organic (2 short-form video scripts)

Brand voice. Hook in the first 1–2 seconds (TikTok kills weak openers fast).

**UTM:** the TikTok bio holds one external link. Tag it per script and swap when posting:
`https://www.trouttricks.com/the-drop.html?utm_source=tiktok&utm_medium=organic-social&utm_campaign=drop-launch&utm_content={angle}`

- Script 1: `utm_content=pov-spinney-spawn`
- Script 2: `utm_content=direct-mastermind-pitch`

**General TikTok craft:**
- 9:16 vertical. Subtitle every line.
- 30–60 sec sweet spot for educational outdoor content.
- Music: a low-energy ambient track or no music (your voice carries it). Avoid trending sounds that don't fit the brand — they tank watch-time.
- Cover frame: use a still that shows your face + a fish or fly. Faces convert.

## SCRIPT 1 — POV: post-spawn Spinney (45 sec)

**Hook angle:** the viewer is the angler struggling. POV format is high engagement on TikTok for niche outdoor content.

[0:00 — TIGHT SHOT, you turned to camera, lake behind you, slight wind]
"POV: you're trying to figure out post-spawn Spinney."

[0:03 — CUT to B-ROLL: indicator drifting on the water, slow chop]
"You've watched the YouTube videos. You know how to tie a chironomid."

[0:08 — BACK TO FACE]
"And the bite window keeps shifting on you. Last week 14 feet was the move. This week 14 feet is dead."

[0:15 — B-ROLL: fly box close-up, finger pointing at different patterns]
"It's not the fly. It's not the leader. It's the depth column. And nothing on YouTube tells you what's happening on Spinney *this* week."

[0:24 — STAND-UP, hold a fly to camera]
"That gap — between general knowledge and right-now knowledge — is what burns trips."

[0:30 — CLOSER SHOT]
"So I built a working group. Ten serious CO stillwater anglers, bi-weekly call, members bring intel. Mastermind format, not a webinar. We pool what we're seeing and work problems together."

[0:42 — CLOSE OUT]
"Founding 10 at $20 a month locked for life. Link in bio. Tight lines."

[On-screen text at the end, 3 sec hold]: "trouttricks.com/the-drop"

**Caption (TikTok caption ≤ 2,200 chars but shorter works better):**

## What to do with comments

- **Real questions** about CO stillwater fishing: answer in the comment thread. Don't pivot to "join The Drop" in the reply. Just answer. The answer IS the proof of value.
- **"Where do I sign up"**: reply with "link in bio 🪝" and pin it.
- **Skeptics / "$20/mo for what?"**: don't defend. Reply: "fair. It's a mastermind — bi-weekly call, real intel, ten anglers. If that's not your speed, no sweat." Then move on.
- **Trolls**: ignore. Don't engage. TikTok's algorithm reads engagement-rate, not engagement-quality — feeding trolls boosts their reach AND lowers your perceived audience quality.

Pin the strongest 1–2 comments after each video drops — usually a real angler asking a smart question.
