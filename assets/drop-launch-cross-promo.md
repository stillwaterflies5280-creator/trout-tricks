# Drop launch — Cross-promo outreach (5–10 CO/Rocky Mountain partners)

Goal: reach the CO stillwater audience through partners whose audience overlaps but who don't compete with Trout Tricks's fly tier. The pitch is reciprocal — they share with their audience, you share with yours.

**UTM** (when sending tracked links to a partner):
`https://www.trouttricks.com/the-drop.html?utm_source={partner-slug}&utm_medium=cross-promo&utm_campaign=drop-launch&utm_content=partner-outreach`

Example: for an outreach to Voormi, the link becomes `?utm_source=voormi&utm_medium=cross-promo&...`.

## Outreach list — 7 candidates ranked by fit

### 1. Voormi (Pagosa Springs, CO) — outdoor wool apparel
- **Why fit:** Colorado-rooted, premium outdoor brand, audience = serious outdoor enthusiasts (skiers, hunters, anglers). Audience overlap likely 30–40% with CO fly anglers.
- **Why non-competing:** wool baselayers / jackets, no fly fishing gear. Pure audience play.
- **Ask:** Instagram Story shout / newsletter mention in their next outdoor-CO drop.
- **Reciprocal offer:** Trout Tricks members get 10% off Voormi via Drop member discount code, OR shout Voormi in The Drop welcome email as a "what to wear when you're on the water all morning" callout.
- **Outreach channel:** Their team email or @-mention on IG. Voormi's founder Dustin English is active on social.

### 2. Big Agnes (Steamboat Springs, CO) — tents / sleeping bags / camp gear
- **Why fit:** Colorado outdoor brand with strong fly fishing audience overlap (anglers who camp for multi-day stillwater trips).
- **Why non-competing:** camping gear, not fishing.
- **Ask:** newsletter mention or blog feature ("guest pick: a CO fly tier we follow").
- **Reciprocal offer:** content trade — Trout Tricks blog post mentioning Big Agnes tent setup for North Park / Delaney Buttes camping; or a Drop guest-call appearance from a Big Agnes ambassador who fishes.
- **Outreach channel:** marketing@bigagnes.com or LinkedIn DM to their CMO.

### 3. RIO Products (Idaho — fly lines, leaders, tippet)
- **Why fit:** fly fishing-specific brand, massive audience, premium positioning, doesn't sell tied flies.
- **Why non-competing:** they sell line + leader, you sell flies. Adjacent gear stack.
- **Ask:** ambassador-style mention on their social or in a Drop-targeted email blast.
- **Reciprocal offer:** trade content — Trout Tricks blog post on indicator-and-leader setup using RIO products, in exchange for a tying-tier shout in their next newsletter.
- **Outreach channel:** RIO is owned by Far Bank Enterprises — DM their pro-staff coordinator or use the press contact on their site.

### 4. Cheeky Fishing (Norwood, MA — fly reels)
- **Why fit:** fly-specific, premium positioning, podcast (The Drift) with serious-angler audience.
- **Why non-competing:** reels, not flies.
- **Ask:** guest spot on The Drift podcast OR sponsored mention in their newsletter.
- **Reciprocal offer:** the podcast spot pays itself (no swap needed) — they get content, you get a 30-minute infomercial in front of their audience. If they want a swap, offer to design a custom Trout Tricks fly set tuned for the lakes Cheeky's CO ambassadors fish.
- **Outreach channel:** Their podcast booking address — usually @cheekyfishing on IG or podcast@cheekyfishing.com style.

### 5. Trout Unlimited Colorado (Denver) — conservation nonprofit
- **Why fit:** literally the institutional voice for CO fly fishing. Audience overlap 80%+. Mission alignment (catch-and-release, anti-hot-spot-leaking, conservation ethos).
- **Why non-competing:** they don't sell anything. They're a 501(c)(3).
- **Ask:** chapter-level newsletter mention ("local fly tier we support"), member-event speaking spot, or blog feature.
- **Reciprocal offer:** Trout Tricks donates a fly set per CO TU chapter banquet auction (cost ~$25-50 of inventory, value $100+ to the auction). Trade with longevity — banquet circuit runs every winter.
- **Outreach channel:** Reach out to CO TU state director or specific chapter coordinators (South Platte, Cutthroat, Greenback chapters are highest fit). Email on tucolorado.org.

### 6. Howler Brothers (Austin, TX) — outdoor apparel with fly fishing tilt
- **Why fit:** outdoor apparel brand with explicit fly fishing audience. Their merch shows up at fly shows. National audience.
- **Why non-competing:** apparel, not flies.
- **Ask:** newsletter feature or IG Story shout.
- **Reciprocal offer:** Trout Tricks blog post mentioning Howler shirts as good wading-day apparel; or guest content trade.
- **Outreach channel:** marketing@howlerbros.com or DM their social team.

### 7. Postcard Press Colorado / Mountain Living-style local publications
- **Why fit:** local Colorado outdoor lifestyle publications with print readership in your geo. Lower reach than a brand but higher trust per impression.
- **Why non-competing:** they publish content; they don't compete with anything.
- **Ask:** featured-business profile or a "5 questions with a CO fly tier" interview.
- **Reciprocal offer:** Trout Tricks promotes the article in the Crew newsletter + on IG. Most local pubs prize cross-promotion they can quote in advertiser pitches.
- **Outreach channel:** editorial@ contacts on their masthead.

## How to actually send these

1. **Personalize the bracketed bits.** No copy-paste. The "one specific thing" sentence in P1 is the difference between a reply and a delete.
2. **Send 2 per week max.** Cross-promo email goes from "thoughtful outreach" to "spam blast" the moment you're sending 5+ in a day.
3. **Subject line variants** to A/B if you've got volume:
   - "Quick one from a Colorado fly tier — possible cross-promo"
   - "[Brand name] x Trout Tricks — Colorado outdoor cross-promo idea"
   - "Following Trout Tricks — a 30-second pitch for [first name]"
4. **Follow up once if no reply within 7–10 days.** Single nudge: "Following up in case this slipped — happy to drop it if not a fit." Then move on.
5. **Track in a simple sheet:** brand · contact · date sent · date followed up · response · outcome. Same template as the DM tracking sheet.

## Realistic conversion expectations

Cold partner outreach converts at maybe 10–20%. So 7 sends = 1–2 partnerships if you're lucky. The good news: even one partnership at the right scale can drive more waitlist signups than 30 days of organic. Pick the top 3–4 candidates (Voormi, Big Agnes, CO TU, RIO) and personalize hard.

The CO TU outreach is the highest-leverage long-tail play — banquet-circuit appearances every winter compound across years.
