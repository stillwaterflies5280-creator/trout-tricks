# Drop launch — DM script for top 10 engaged fans (Step 3)

**Personalize the opener per recipient.** The template below has bracketed placeholders for the personal touch. The bones of the message stay the same; the opener changes based on what you know about each person.

**Send these one at a time, not in bulk.** Spread across a week. FB flags bulk-DMs as spam and the platform's "limited account" warning will tank your reach.

## Template

> Hey [first name] — saw your [post about / fish from / question on] [specific lake or pattern — Antero / Spinney / 11 Mile / your recent Wyoming trip / etc.]. Always like seeing your stuff land in the feed.
>
> Quick one — kicking around an idea and wanted a gut check from people who actually fish CO stillwaters at a real cadence. Mind giving me 30 seconds?
>
> The idea: small mastermind. Bi-weekly call with ~10 serious CO stillwater anglers. Open-floor format — members bring intel (conditions, recent trips, rigs that aren't earning), we work problems together. Less webinar, more "ten of us in a room comparing notes."
>
> Selfish version of why: my regulars keep asking the same questions and I want a forum where the answers compound instead of getting buried in threads.
>
> Two questions if you've got a sec:
> 1. Does that fill a gap for you, or sound like more noise?
> 2. If yes — anything you'd want in or out?
>
> No pressure to join — I'm in research mode. If you do want a seat though, founding 10 is $20/mo locked for life:
> trouttricks.com/the-drop.html?utm_source=facebook&utm_medium=dm&utm_campaign=drop-launch&utm_content=personal-outreach
>
> Either way — appreciate the input. Tight lines.
> — Thomas

## Opener variants (pick one per recipient)

These cover the most likely fan profiles. Mix-and-match so DMs don't feel scripted:

| Profile | Opener line |
|---|---|
| Recently posted a fish photo | "Hey [name] — that **[rainbow / brown / cutbow]** you posted from **[lake]** was a unit. Always like seeing your stuff." |
| Asked a chironomid / pattern question in a group | "Hey [name] — saw your question about **[chironomid color / depth / leech retrieve]** in [group name]. Good thread." |
| Posted a Wyoming or out-of-state trip | "Hey [name] — that **[Wyoming / Utah / wherever]** trip looked solid. The [specific takeaway from their post] stuck with me." |
| Long-time customer who's bought flies | "Hey [name] — hope the **[Chocolate Gold / Snow Cone / whatever they bought]** is earning. Quick one for you when you have a sec." |
| Active in your DMs already, comment-replier | "Hey [name] — you've been a regular in the comments lately, appreciate it. Quick one — kicking around an idea." |

## What to do with the replies

- **"Yes, fills a gap" + signs up:** they're your evangelists. Thank them, send them The Drop founding spot, send the welcome email manually if Klaviyo flow isn't built yet.
- **"Yes" + doesn't sign up:** ask what's holding them back. Friction is data. Iterate on the landing page from their answer.
- **"No / sounds like noise":** ask why. Don't push. Their objection is the most valuable thing in the conversation.
- **No reply within 5 days:** don't follow up. One nudge per person is the rule. Anything more reads as desperate.

---

## Don't do this

- Don't copy-paste 10 of these in one sitting. FB sees the pattern.
- Don't make the link the first thing in the DM. Lead with the personal opener.
- Don't apologize or hedge ("sorry to bother you," "I know this is random"). Reads weak.
- Don't pitch in the opener. Ask first, offer second.
- Don't @-mention other people in the DM to "prove" the idea. Awkward.
- Don't link to the homepage. Always the UTM-tagged /the-drop.html so DM-source conversions show up clean in GA4.

## Tracking

After you send the 10 DMs, log each in a sheet (or just a note):
- Name · group where you found them · date sent · opener variant used · response (Yes / No / No reply) · whether they signed up
- This gives you the conversion rate for personal outreach. Likely far higher than the FB group posts — but only if you do the personalization right.
