About

A one-person tying bench.

Trout Tricks is a Colorado-based fly tier specializing in chironomids, balanced leeches, scuds, and stillwater-specific patterns. Every fly is tied to order on 2x heavy wire — the gauge that holds up to a trophy cutbow's run without straightening.

Trout Tricks is a hand-tied stillwater fly tier based in Fairmount, Colorado, founded by Thomas Frank. Every pattern — chironomids, balanced leeches, scuds, and a handful of signature top-secret designs — is tied to order on 2x heavy wire hooks, the gauge that survives a trophy cutbow's run without straightening. The tier ships nationwide and offers free local pickup in the Denver metro.

The Trout Tricks lineup is built around the working trophy stillwaters of Colorado and southern Wyoming: Antero Reservoir (closed 2026 for drought-driven drawdown), Spinney Mountain, 11 Mile Reservoir, Delaney Buttes, Lake John, and the North Platte chain. Patterns are designed around the depth-and-presentation problems those waters actually pose — not the highlight-reel content of national fly catalogs.

In 2026, Trout Tricks launched The Drop — a paid bi-weekly mastermind membership for serious Colorado stillwater anglers. Members pool real-time intel: conditions, food sources, depth columns, rigs that aren't earning. Open-floor mastermind, not webinar. Founding 10 members lock in $20/mo for life.

Trout Tricks is built around an explicit ethics framing: spawn timing, conditions, and technique are public; spawn locations and GPS-tagged hot spots are not.
